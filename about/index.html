First things first... Who am I?

Ever since I got my first computer in the early 2000's, I began learning how they worked. While I may have broken a couple of them (and making my dad quite upset) during my self-teachings, the books I read (to expand my understanding of these wonderful machines), at that time, where not sufficient. When I first surfed the internet (and yes, when AOL was very popular), my knowledge of computers increased and I eventually taught myself a bit of HTML and CSS. Little did I know the road that was waiting ahead for me.

Java... My first programming language experience.

The Computer Science Department at San Jose State University (as of 2014) mainly uses Java as its programming language. While I may have really enjoyed this language during my first semesters, I began to be aware of other languages. While attending this higher institution, I was introduced to UNIX Systems, how to use the terminal, and learned many languages I have never heard of. Computers simply keep amazing me.

Where am I now? JabbaScrypt!

In the fall semester of 2013, in my Compiler Design course, my group and I created JabbaScrypt: A very simple, light-weight procedural programming language. As of this moment, this has been my best programming project yet. Not only is creating a compiler interesting, but making a new language is just... well, it fills me up with joy! I am now currently waiting for my first opportunity as a professional Computer Programmer / Software Engineer.
